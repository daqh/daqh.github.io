# Valerio Di Pasquale

> PhD Student in Computer Science and Information Technology at Università degli Studi di Salerno. Research focuses on artificial intelligence for complex networks, with particular attention to graph and hypergraph generative models, motif analysis, link prediction, and graph-based representation learning. Also interested in combining graph models with large language models for structured-data reasoning.

## About

Valerio Di Pasquale is a PhD student based in Salerno, Italy. His work sits at the intersection of machine learning and network science. Alongside research, he has professional experience in web design and development and a long-standing freelance background building applications for individuals and small businesses.

## Research Interests

Current directions: generative models for graphs and hypergraphs, link prediction and hyperlink prediction, motif analysis in real-world networks, graph representation learning, geometric deep learning and graph neural networks.

Broader interests: large language models for structured data reasoning, graph-to-text generation and question answering over graphs, diffusion models and variational autoencoders, applications to biology and social systems, interpretable AI methods for network analysis.

## Projects

- [Parallel Deep Generative Model for Hypergraph](https://github.com/daqh/parallel_deep_hypergraph_generation): deep variational autoencoder for generating synthetic hypergraphs in parallel, implemented in Python with PyTorch.
